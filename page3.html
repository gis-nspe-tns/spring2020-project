Mapping Hotspots: Gloabal Conflict Zones

This spatial analysis project defines armed conflict geographies as those that are contested by at least two parties - one of which is a governmental force resulting in at least 25 deaths in one calendar year. Varied spatial distribution of conflict rates occur across both dense and more rural human geographies. This spatial analysis finds that small scale conflict hot spots are largely located in Africa, the Middle East and Central and the coastal plains of Southern America; and these conflicts occur throughout both larger scale urban and rural geographies..

A spatial analysis of Greece's Contemporary Migratory Flows

The 2015 Refugee Crisis has become internationally recognized and lamented by media and international humanitarian workers. The narratives explaining this 2015 crisis allow for the misconception that the crisis has ended as well as began as suddenly as the start of January 2015. Greece, with its southeastern location in Europe has been largely impacted by these population flows coming from both land and sea borders. The flow of vulnerable people to Greece has increased yearly since 2015 (although 2017 saw slightly fewer numbers to 2016). This GIS analysis results in cartographic representaion that demonstrates the continuing increase of flows to Greece to recompense the misconceptions of the past, one year and crisis.
